Before the DAT: Introduction


In this series of pages, we will explore essential aspects of preparing for dental school, providing valuable insights and practical advice. The subsequent sections will delve into various topics, including Choosing a Major, Picking Courses, Academics, Volunteering/Shadowing, and Extracurricular Activities/Work/Research. Let's take a closer look at what each section entails:

Choosing a Major:


Choosing the right major is an important decision for aspiring dental students. This section will explore the best majors for dental school, taking into account their relevance and benefits. We will examine degree statistics, providing insights into the percentage of students who choose specific majors. Additionally, we will discuss the advantages of pursuing minor degrees and how they can enhance your dental school application.

Picking Courses:


To gain admission to dental school, certain prerequisites must be fulfilled. In this section, we will dive into the dental school prerequisites, explaining what they are and why they matter. We will guide you on when to take these courses and highlight specific requirements of different dental schools. Emphasizing the importance of sequencing courses correctly, we will demonstrate how the order in which you take these courses can assist in preparing for the Dental Admission Test (DAT).

Academics:


Academic performance plays a vital role in the dental school application process. In this section, we will explore the different types of GPAs (Grade Point Averages) that dental schools consider. We will provide you with insightful dental school statistics, including GPA trends and acceptance rates, allowing you to gain a comprehensive understanding of the academic landscape. Additionally, we will discuss the implications of withdrawing from or retaking courses and how these decisions can impact your dental school prospects.

Volunteering/Shadowing:


Volunteering and shadowing experiences are crucial for aspiring dentists. This section will guide you through the various types of volunteering and shadowing opportunities that can bolster your dental school application. We will provide statistical insights into schools' requirements regarding these experiences, empowering you to make informed decisions. Furthermore, we will offer valuable advice on finding volunteering and shadowing opportunities and provide email templates to facilitate your communication with potential opportunities.

Extracurricular Activities/Work/Research:


Beyond academics, dental schools seek well-rounded applicants who are actively engaged in extracurricular activities, work experiences, and research. This section will underline the significance of involvement in these areas and how they contribute to your overall profile. We will provide guidance on how to explore and participate in extracurricular activities, secure relevant work experiences, and engage in research opportunities. Understanding the importance of each aspect will enable you to showcase your diverse skill set to dental school admissions committees.

By delving into these critical areas of dental school preparation, this guide aims to equip you with comprehensive knowledge and practical strategies. Whether you are just starting your journey or looking to enhance your application, "Before the DAT" will serve as a valuable resource, empowering you to navigate the complexities of the dental school application process.

*All statistics for required courses, GPA school averages, and other requirements are researched and obtained from the 2021 school year*
